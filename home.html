IIT-JEE Preparation

What is JEE Main exam for? The JEE-Main, which replaces AIEEE, is for admission to the National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), and some other colleges designated as "centrally funded technical institutes" (CFTIs).

Why IIT?
What makes IITs different from other institutions is that they pull our focus towards how we can apply what we have studied in the real world. The assignments encourage the technique of ‘brainstorming’ and there is a free sense of interaction between students and professors on the feasibility of projects. Adding to this, the laboratories contain world-class equipment and there are constant quizzes, competitions and exams to keep us on our toes. We get our due of recognition with students winning trips to some of the world’s most precious universities for international conferences, global competitions and placements at world-class companies. Students also get an opportunity to meet eminent professors, scientists and Nobel laureates while also being made eligible for fellowships, scholarships and financial assistance on projects. Many even take part in student-exchange and internship programmes with the world’s best universities including MIT and Harvard. Students are also encouraged to pursue their hobbies and extra-curricular activities in order to not miss out on overall development. In short, the students do not have to search for opportunities; the IITs present them with such breaks every other day.
IIT's Admission Process and Selection Process
JEE Main and Advances examinations gauge a student's efficiency in three subjects, namely Mathematics, Chemistry, and Physics. After answering six papers in JEE advanced, IITs prepare 9 rank lists based on reservation and caste parameters.Separate lists are prepared for scheduled castes, scheduled tribes, general category, other backward classes and persons with disabilities. Some students are listed in a preparatory courses rank list and are mentored by IITs, before being offered an opportunity to enroll in a course with time-lag. The country is divided into seven zones by IITs, making it easier to monitor JEE Advanced and the process of joining IIT. IIT Bombay zone, which includes Kota, Rajasthan, produces most toppers and a majority of overall qualified students. It is followed by IIT Madras zone and IIT Delhi zone in the aforementioned parameters. Other than IITs, qualified students are eligible for admission at Indian School of Mines, Dhanbad. The cumulative number of seats in all these institutes is approximately 11,000.
A brief introduction to IIT Mains and Advanced
Students generally start preparing for IIT JEE from 10th or 11th standard, even though they appear for said JEE examination with 12th class board examinations.
It is a two-step process and only on qualifying both levels with good scores does a student become eligible to join one of these 23 institutes.
The first level known as JEE Mains is a preliminary examination, wherein qualifying students to become eligible for answering JEE Advanced, the second level examination. In order to answer JEE Advanced students are required to qualify JEE Mains with a good rank.
Earlier, 12th class marks contributed to JEE Mains score. However, from 2017 the sole prerequisite is to score above 75%, as actual marks are no longer taken into account when calculating Mains rank. Students who have answered their 12th standard examinations can appear for JEE Advanced in the same year.
Life of students at IIT
A popular belief is that students at IITs are only concerned with their studies; however, that is simply not true. IITians are involved in many activities, making their life at IITs an enjoyable and holistic experience. Apart from attending classes, students proactively participate in activities ranging from sports to arts to other diverse fields. Some of these fields are robotics clubs, software or Android development groups or NCC and NSS. While earning their degrees, students often participate in projects and conduct research under professors. Moreover, they contribute to the development of their department, institute and their preferred field. Simultaneously, they strive to earn scholarships and accolades through these activities. During the day focus remains strictly on academics, however after 6 p.m. students are focused on developing as individuals by utilizing state-of-the-art facilties. Since most students put all extracurricular activities on hold for two years to prepare for JEE, they indulge in all-around self development.
